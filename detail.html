Introduction

Ancient Egypt was a civilization of ancient North Africa, concentrated along the lower reaches of the Nile River, situated in the place that is now the country Egypt. Ancient Egyptian civilization followed prehistoric Egypt and coalesced around 3100 BC with the political unification of Upper and Lower Egypt under Menes (often identified with Narmer).

Key Achievements

  • Construction of the Great Pyramids and the Sphinx
  • Development of hieroglyphic writing
  • Advancements in mathematics and astronomy
  • Sophisticated irrigation and agricultural systems
  • Pioneering techniques in art and architecture
3100 BCE Year
Unification of Egypt

The unification of Upper and Lower Egypt under King Menes (also known as Narmer) marks the beginning of Ancient Egyptian civilization. This period saw the establishment of the first dynasty and the foundation of Memphis as the capital.

2686 BCE Old
Age of the Pyramids

e Old Kingdom period, also known as the Age of the Pyramids, saw the construction of the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Great Sphinx. This era was marked by strong central government and significant advancements in architecture and art.

21550 BCE New
Imperial Egypt

The New Kingdom period saw Egypt reach the peak of its power. This era included the reigns of famous pharaohs like Hatshepsut, Thutmose III, Akhenaten, and Ramesses II. It was a time of great wealth, imperial expansion, and monumental building projects.

664 BCE Period
Last Native Dynasties

The Late Period marked the last era of Ancient Egypt's independence. It was a time of foreign invasions and internal strife, but also saw a renaissance in Egyptian art and culture. The period ended with the conquest of Egypt by Alexander the Great in 332 BCE.
